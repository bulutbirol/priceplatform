# Leaf Category Visual Explainer Design

## Goal

Turn every leaf product category into a useful visual explainer rather than a term index.

## Page structure

1. Product introduction.
2. System map: the product in the center and four category-specific core systems around it.
3. Detailed core-system notes explaining purpose, price effect, advantage, and drawback.
4. Three-step buying checklist derived from the product's own technology profile.
5. Full price factors, term catalog, and guides.

## Accuracy

The visual and checklist must use only terms already assigned to that product. They may not invent components or reuse another category's terms. If fewer than four terms exist, the visual renders only the available systems.

## Visual language

Use a restrained technical drawing: numbered nodes, thin connector lines, strong typography, and short annotations. The diagram is code-native HTML/CSS, accessible as structured text, and works in dark mode.

## Validation

Component tests verify that a product map uses its supplied terms and exposes advantage/disadvantage text. Existing category tests verify the explainer appears on real leaf pages.
